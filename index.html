Space Configuration — 36×38 ft Recommendation

Recommended Allocation (~1,368 sq ft)

Rationale

Single large flexible main space with modular walls (Paxton's movable-wall approach) outperforms a multi-small-room layout at this footprint. Prioritize equipment quality and wall modularity over subdividing into many themed rooms — 36×38 ft is below the threshold where heavy multi-room segmentation pays off. The industry trend toward multi-format studios (photo + video + podcast in one booking) makes a flexible main floor with an audio-treated corner the optimal configuration. Add an interchangeable accent wall panel system (4–6 color options, like Bottoms Up Studio's 6-wall palette) for visual variety without permanent renovation.

  • Main shooting floor: 600–700 sq ft (non-negotiable)
  • Equipment / prop storage: 200+ sq ft
  • Makeup + changing room: 100+ sq ft
  • Pantry / break area: 100+ sq ft
  • Washrooms: 100+ sq ft
  • Optional: podcast booth or mirror wall (~50 sq ft) — pick one, not both

      Summary Verdict

      Position BOE as a premium-accessible, multi-format content studio filling the ₹15,000–₹24,000/day sweet spot between budget and premium tiers. Photography/video are the core anchor (12 of 14 studios). Podcast is the fastest-growing gap (6 of 14 offer it — India podcast market at 27.1% CAGR). Include a dedicated audio-treated podcast corner, interchangeable accent wall panels, and modular walls enabling photo→video→podcast pivots in a single booking. Add a "Quick-Shoot" hourly slot (₹1,500–₹2,000/hr) inspired by the Click OK/Sugarcane model to capture spontaneous social content demand. Avoid yoga/fitness/dance as headline activities (zero market validation across 14 studios). India's creator economy at $15B (2026) provides the macro tailwind — execute on format flexibility, transparent pricing, and equipment depth.
